Birkdale was the first project I had the pleasure to work when I started at Gaunt Francis Architects.

The initial task was to meet with the heritage consultant on site and collect as much information as I could. My approach was to create a huge catalogue using the floor plans, photos from the site visit and linked them with the annotations I had.

Right after, the idea was to detect and expose the overall damage of the building as the fire consumed almost half of it. This was key to keep working afterward on the listed building itself.

The masterplan design was proposed by the lead architect and I was assisting to prepare the planning submission. The main job at this stage was to focus myself on Revit to prepare the blocks floor plans but also schemes and views for the public consultation. All of this was done with a colleague as a great team work experience.
